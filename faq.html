FAQ

1. What do SAFE / CAUTION / DANGER / RUG actually mean?

The verdict is a band on the 1000-point score, with hard overrides:

SAFE (score ≥900, ≥5 sources active): no critical signals across all 7 layers.
CAUTION (600-899): at least one risk signal but nothing critical — e.g. concentrated top-10 holders, mild wash-trading ratio, very young token.
DANGER (350-599): multiple risk signals or one critical signal partially mitigated by maturity.
RUG (<350 or Safe Gate triggered): hard kill — LP open, honeypot, mint/freeze active, or deceptive name.

2. Can a SAFE token still rug?

Yes. Antares is a probabilistic risk screen, not a guarantee. We can detect what 6 independent sources surface at scan time — novel exploits, coordinated dumps via fresh wallets, or social-engineering attacks may not register.

The Time-to-Rug ring and Outcome Histogram are indicative, derived from a heuristic profile match against historical clusters — they are not validated forecasts. See the Terms for the full disclaimer.

3. Why is this token flagged DANGER when it looks fine?

False positives are real and unavoidable. Common causes:

• LP not burned but token is mature & liquid — we still penalise unburned LP because the dev can rug at any time, even if the project looks legitimate (e.g. Fartcoin, DeFade). The established-bonus rule unlocks SAFE for tokens 30+ days old with 50k+ holders + GoPlus clean.

• Volume/liquidity ratio above 5× — flagged as wash-trading risk even on legitimate flash-pool venues where that ratio is structural.

• Deceptive name detection sometimes catches generic financial terms ("Vanguard Capital" matching "Vanguard"). We’re tightening this.

If you think a verdict is wrong, open an issue with the token CA.

4. What data does Antares collect about me?

None personal. The API receives the contract address you scan plus standard HTTPS metadata (IP, user-agent) used for rate-limiting only. We don’t store profiles, we don’t correlate scans across users, we don’t sell anything.

Full disclosure with retention windows per system: /privacy.

5. Why does the extension require activeTab and tabs permissions?

To detect Solana contract addresses on the supported trading platforms (DexScreener, pump.fun, Axiom, Photon, Birdeye, GeckoTerminal, GMGN). The Extension reads the URL and visible page DOM on those sites only. It does not read form fields, credentials, cookies, or browsing history.

6. How fast is a scan?

Typical: 3–5 seconds end-to-end (6 concurrent upstream calls + AI summary + insider graph + scoring). Cached scans return in under 100ms.

Slower than RugCheck (<1s, single source) by design — we trade speed for depth (7 layers + clustering + holder activity).

7. Multichain support? Ethereum / BSC / Base?

Solana only for v1. Multichain is on the roadmap but not committed for the first public release. Phase 0 (DexScreener + GoPlus EVM) is the entry point; full feature parity (Critical Actors / Holder Activity / Outcome Stats on EVM) is multi-week work.

8. Is the AI summary reliable?

It’s a Google Gemini 2.5-flash output grounded in the actual scan data (verdict, top flags, holder concentration, vol/liq, age). It can occasionally hallucinate or misinterpret — treat it as plain-English context, not authoritative analysis. The raw flag list and verdict are the ground truth.

If Gemini is unreachable, a local fallback dictionary covers the 12 most common flag explanations so the AI Verdict card never goes empty.

9. The Time-to-Rug ring says “4h 12m median”. Is that accurate?

It’s indicative, not predictive. The number is a heuristic profile match against historical clusters of comparable launches. Hardcoded distributions per profile (fast-rug / high-risk / slow-death). The corpus-based KNN matcher (the J3-J5 backtest harness) is in development — until then, treat the ring as “tokens like this typically die in this window” not “this specific token will die at 4h 12m.”

10. How do I report a false positive / false negative / bug?

Open an issue at github.com/COMEALAMAISONGROUPE/antares-extension/issues with: the token CA, what verdict you got, what you expected, and the timestamp of the scan (visible in the extension’s metric tab as requestId).

Security issues should use Private Vulnerability Reporting per SECURITY.md — do not include exploit details in a public issue.
